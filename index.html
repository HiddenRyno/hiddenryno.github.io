DarkWeb

This is a hidden layer of the internet, not accessible through regular search engines. To enter this hidden world, one needs special software, and the most popular among these is Tor (The Onion Router).

  • Tor: Free software for anonymous internet use.
  • Global network of servers hides user's location and activity.
  • Interesting Fact: Named 'The Onion Router' due to its data layering technique.

How Tor Works...

Let’s dive into how Tor actually works. It's based on a principle called 'onion routing.

  • Onion Routing: Data encrypted in multiple layers.
  • Each server (relay) removes one encryption layer.
  • No single point knows both origin and destination.

Potential Risks...

While Tor is a powerful tool for online privacy, it’s not without its risks and limitations

  • Misconception: Tor is solely for illegal activities.
  • Reality: Tor is a tool for privacy, not illegal actions.
  • Legal and ethical use is paramount.
  • Tor can be slower than regular browsing.
  • Some websites may not be Tor-compatible.
  • Risk of accidental personal information leakage.

Security Considerations...

In terms of security, Tor is quite robust. It employs multiple layers of encryption and routes data through a network that lacks a central control point, which protects against tracking and surveillance

  • Multiple layers of encryption.
  • Decentralized network prevents tracking.
  • Offers significant anonymity and privacy.
